Strategy

Identifying Consumer Segment and their unfullfilled needs.

The first step of our producut devlopemnt process involved choosing a general idea of what kind of product we wanted to create. Our team was back and forth on a bunch of ideas, from drinks to mobile apps, but ultimatly we felt that . With the dog toy industry in mind, we had to uncover some of consumers pain points when using toys with their dogs. To acheive this, we conducted simple interviews with dog owners, asking open ended questions such as:

  • How often do you typically play with your dog?
  • What factors prevent you from playing with your dog?
  • What do you dislike most about dog toys in general?
  • How often are you out of the house away from your dog, and how important is it for you to find varoius ways to keep your dog entertained?

With this information we relaized there was a lot of pain points when it comes to consumers and entertaining their furry companion. We found out that most people are willing to do anyhting to make their dog happy, but it is often difficult to find the time an energy to go out and play with your dog everyday. This allowed us to focus our ideas on something to help consumers who feel the same.

Creating Products to Help our Target Market

After identifying consumers needs, we had a better understanding the gaps our product should fill in the consumers product use. With this, my team and I were able to genererate hundreds of ideas through several brainstorming sessions. However, as a team we had to ultimatly decide on our two favorite ideas. These ideas were of course the Hide & Treat, as well as the "Canine Crafter", which was an AI tool that would help consumers create a dog toy prefect for their dog, being both generated and 3D printed by us. Though I strongly disliked this idea, I knew I could not let my personel prefernces get in the way, as these two products would go head to head through a large qaulatative survey. To effectively test our products, we had to create produduct statements for each of our products which followed certain guidelines to reflect the standard of product statments in our industry.

Before our large survey, we had to first conduct qaulatative research through surveys on each of our products, mainly to understand where we could make improvements in the specific product allowing us to improve the product statement, which would be used in our quatative survey. To conduct these interviews we went to dog centric locations such as dog parks and pet stores, asking the consumer give us feedback on our current product ideas. Along with the participants genreal thoughts on the idea, we also asked some specific qustions regarding features of the product itself such as what scents they would prefer, and what material/color they would expect the final product to be.

Conducting Surveys to Decide on our Product

With our product statements for both "Hide & Treat" and "Canine Crafter" finalized we moved on to creating/condcucting our surveys using Qualtrics. To avoid any potnetial bias, a participant would be able to provide answers on only one of our two products. This would allow the participant to give feedback on the product, without unknowingly comparing it to the other product idea they saw. Each survey also followed the same layout which consisted of the following:

  1. The product concept, and how the product is meant to be used allowing the participants to anser the questions
  2. Open ended question on what the participant specifiaclly likes about the product
  3. Open ended question on what the participant specifiaclly dislikes about the product
  4. The likelihood they would purchase the product in general
  5. The likelihood they would purchase the product at a specific price point, if they do not answer at either "definitly would purchase" or "probably would purchase", the participant would be given the same question with a lower specified price point.
  6. What would be their maximum price point, and what price would be so low that they would question the quality of the product
  7. Open ended feedback on what they believe is the main benift
  8. How unique the product is, and whether or not they think it would actually work
  9. General questions on the participants demograghic

With this information our team had all the knowledge we needed to move forward into the project. The data did not only consist of the participants general fedback on each idea and what people liked better on average, but also the necessry data to create the price for each product using a formula we were given in class. This would allow us to determine the financail feasablitly of launching each product. The Final Product

Here came the most important choice in the project, which was choosing between the AI driven "Canine Crafter" and the "Hide & Treat". Though the survey results suggested that the "Hide & Treat" was a better option, one team member still wanted to go forth with the Canine Crafter. I respected his opinion as it was his own product idea, and he also was the only other team member that that showed a lot of care for the project throughout the year. Our team was evenly split between the two options. This when I stepped up and gave my genuine thoughts, as I believed an AI that creates and 3D prints perfect dog toys seemed extremely unrealistic, but also just felt like a cop-out, and did not display any characterisitcs of a true innovation pointing to the survey data that expressed similar ideas. I did feel bad advocating for my own product idea over my teamates, I assured both him and my team that this would be the most rewarding outcome, as I would be putting in the extra work of logo and package design, for my vision for the brand was so vivid at that point.

With our final product idea finalized, we were now ready to put our focus on the necessary details needed to launch our product.First was determining the contriibution margin for our prodcut, which involved generating a price using our survey data, then looking at several websites to determine how much materials and manufacturing would cost us per unit. With that had to determine the sales we could expect on prodcut launch, in which we had several ways of calculating, but ultimatly we felt that a bottom up forcasting approach was best, as it would most accuratly represent the number of sales.
